Momo - Steamed Dumplings

Momos are a type of steamed filled dumpling in Tibetan and Nepali cuisine that is also popular in neighbouring Bhutan, Bangladesh, and India. The majority of Tibetan momos are half-moon in shape like jiaozi, while Nepali momos are normally round like baozi.

30

Minutes

4

Servings

Medium

Difficulty

These delicious dumplings are perfect as an appetizer, snack, or main course. Serve with spicy tomato chutney or sesame sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 pound ground chicken or vegetables
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour with water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. For the filling, heat oil in a pan and saut√© garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add onions and cook until translucent. Add ground chicken (or vegetables) and cook until done.
  4. Add cabbage, turmeric, cumin, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes until cabbage is tender.
  5. Remove from heat and mix in cilantro. Let the filling cool completely.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).
  7. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle and fold to form a half-moon shape, pinching the edges to seal.
  8. Arrange momos in a steamer lined with parchment paper or cabbage leaves.
  9. Steam for 10-12 minutes until the dough is cooked through.
  10. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
